François Thierry

MEng - MSc - PhD

Diplomaed with a PhD in nano-optoelectronics, I am currently searching for a career opportunity between physics and computer science. I am curious and I like to experiment with code to answer my questions. This is why I am really interested in physical modeling and data visualization.

I recently started to regroup my coding experiments on github and I invite you to visit my repositories overview. My languages of predilection are Python and Web Technologies (HTML/CSS/JS) but I also know Matlab and C/C++ and I have used CAO softwares like Autodesk Inventor.

If you want to know more about my education and research activities, you can consult my resume or my publications and if you would like to contact me, please send me an email.

Curriculum Vitae
2012 - 2015

Doctoral Thesis

I obtained a governmental grant with teaching position at the Aix-Marseille University in France. I conducted my PhD research at the Materials, Microelectronics and Nanosciences Institute of Provence (IM2NP) in the Optoelectronics and Photovoltaics (OPTOPV) Team under the supervision of Pr. F. Flory and Dr. J. Le Rouzo.
The main goals of my research were to calculate the unique properties of quantum nanostructures and to study the optical properties of hybrid organic-inorganic thin-films. I defended on December 14th 2015 with the title Study of semiconductor nanoparticles properties for hybrid solar cells.

2009 - 2012

Master of Engineering & Master of Science

In fall 2009, I started a Master of Engineering in Material Science and Technology at the University of Technology of Troyes (UTT).

For this formation I spent 6 months in 2010 conducting an R&D Internship with the title Characterization and Optimization of a Novel All Electronic Terahertz System at the Fraunhofer Institute for Physical Measurement Techniques (IPM). The goal of the project was to use and optimize the new non-destructive THz imaging system, but I also developed an hyperspectral image processing software using Matlab.

In 2011 I followed simultaneously the Master of Science in Optics and Nanotechnology in the course of a double diploma from the UTT.

In 2012, to conclude my Master Degrees, I went to Innsbrück Unniversity in Austria for a 6 months Research Internship where I performed slit experiments with single photon sources (non-linear crystal and quantum dots) in order to test Born's law.

2007 - 2009

DUT (Technology Degree)

I spent two years preparing my technical degree and I went to the LERM.

1990 - 2007

Until High-School Graduation

I spent my childhood in the Alps before going to Orléans in the center of France where I went to school. In high-school I integrated a European class with a major in German. I learned German and I also had the opportunity to travel for several months in Germany.
